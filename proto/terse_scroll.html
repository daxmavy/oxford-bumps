What the model leans on

A single bump is a coin toss — until a crew shows its pace

Before racing, one day's bump is barely more predictable than a flip. Each day a crew reveals its real speed, so the call sharpens. This is revealed strength, not a hot streak.

Blades

Blades can't be fluked — but they barely repeat

Give every crew identical speed and let luck alone decide: blades almost never happen. They occur about eleven times more often in reality. That gap is genuine pace.

What actually moves a crew

Form carries; a Blue is worth most of a place

Holding starting position fixed, four edges show up and two don't. The strongest read on a crew is its own recent form — at Torpids and last May — and the Oxford Blues in the boat.
